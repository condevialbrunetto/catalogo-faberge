## 4. Historical Context (lido:eventWrap)

### 4.1. The Battle of Kulm (29–30 August 1813)

The **Battle of Kulm** (also known as the Battle of Chlumec) was fought on 29–30 August 1813 near the town of Kulm (now Chlumec) in Bohemia, during the War of the Sixth Coalition against Napoleon Bonaparte. 

The battle was a significant victory for the Allied forces (Russia, Prussia, and Austria) against the French corps commanded by General Dominique Vandamme. The Russian Imperial Guard, including the **Leib-Guard Jäger Regiment** (Лейб-гвардии Егерский полк), played a crucial role in the engagement.

Key facts:
- **Date**: 29–30 August 1813 (Old Style: 17–18 August)
- **Location**: Kulm (Chlumec), Bohemia (now Czech Republic)
- **Outcome**: Decisive Allied victory; General Vandamme captured
- **Significance**: Prevented Napoleon from exploiting his victory at Dresden; turned the tide of the 1813 campaign

### 4.2. The Leib-Guard Jäger Regiment

The **Лейб-гвардии Егерский полк** (Leib-Guard Jäger Regiment) was an elite light infantry unit of the Russian Imperial Guard, established in 1796. The regiment distinguished itself in numerous engagements during the Napoleonic Wars, including:

- Battle of Austerlitz (1805)
- Battle of Friedland (1807)
- Battle of Borodino (1812)
- **Battle of Kulm (1813)**
- Battle of Leipzig (1813)
- Capture of Paris (1814)

The regiment's participation in the Battle of Kulm was particularly notable, and this presentation piece commemorates that service.

## 5. Detailed Description (lido:objectDescriptionWrap)

This extraordinary presentation piece takes the form of a **gilt silver egg** mounted on a tripod base of three stylised artillery shells, resting on a circular platform. The egg functions as a **clock**, with the upper portion featuring a white enamel dial with Roman numerals and a central aperture revealing the gilt brass clockwork mechanism.

### 5.1. Form and Structure

The egg is divided into two main sections:

1. **Upper Section (Clock)**: 
   - White enamel dial ring with Roman numerals (I–XII for hours, additional minute markings)
   - Central aperture exposing the clockwork mechanism
   - Winding key mounted at the apex with decorative ring terminal
   - Hinged construction allowing access to the mechanism

2. **Lower Section (Body)**:
   - Gilt silver ovoid body with polished surface
   - Four Cyrillic inscriptions arranged around the circumference
   - Applied silver Imperial Double-Headed Eagles (at least two visible)
   - Applied silver oak leaf and acorn garlands (symbols of military valour)
   - Engraved date "1813" on the body

3. **Base**:
   - Three artillery shell-shaped feet (symbolic of military service)
   - Circular platform base
   - Additional engraved or applied decoration

### 5.2. Inscriptions (Transcription and Translation)

The egg bears four principal inscriptions in pre-revolutionary Cyrillic orthography:

| Cyrillic Text | Transliteration | English Translation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **ОТЪ ТОВАРИЩЕЙ** | Ot Tovarishchey | From [the] Comrades |
| **ВЪ ПАМЯТЬ** | V Pamyat | In Memory |
| **БИТВЫ ПРИ КУЛЬМѢ** | Bitvy pri Kulme | Of the Battle of Kulm |
| **ЛЕЙБЪ ЕГЕРЕЙ** | Leyb Yegerey | [Of the] Leib-Jäger |
| **1813** | 1813 | 1813 |

The use of the **hard sign (Ъ)** and the **yat (Ѣ)** confirms pre-1918 orthography, consistent with the stated date.

### 5.3. Heraldic Elements

The applied **Imperial Double-Headed Eagle** is rendered in full heraldic form:
- Two crowned heads facing left and right
- Imperial Crown above
- Orb and sceptre in talons
- Shield of St. George (Георгий Победоносец) on breast
- Chain of the Order of St. Andrew

This is the **Greater State Emblem** of the Russian Empire, appropriate for an Imperial Guard presentation piece.

## 7. Comparative Analysis

### 7.1. Related Objects

This presentation egg-clock belongs to a category of **Imperial Russian regimental presentation silver** that flourished during the late 18th and 19th centuries. Comparable objects include:

1. **Regimental kovshi** (traditional drinking vessels) presented to officers
2. **Commemorative cups and goblets** marking battles and anniversaries
3. **Presentation swords and sabres** with similar inscriptions
4. **Fabergé regimental eggs** (later period, 1885–1917)

### 7.2. Stylistic Dating

The object's stylistic features are consistent with **Russian Empire decorative arts of the 1810s–1820s**:

- Neoclassical form (ovoid egg shape)
- Military symbolism (artillery shells, oak leaves)
- Pre-revolutionary Cyrillic orthography
- Quality of gilding and chasing
- Clockwork construction

## 9. Curatorial Analysis (MARIO ANTONIO SOARES VIAL BRUNETTO)

This **Commemorative Egg-Clock of the Battle of Kulm** represents an exceptional example of Imperial Russian military presentation silver. The combination of functional clockwork with commemorative inscription and Imperial heraldry places this object at the intersection of decorative arts, horology, and military history.

The piece is of **museum quality** and of significant historical importance for several reasons:

1. **Historical Significance**: Direct connection to the Napoleonic Wars and the Battle of Kulm, a turning point in the 1813 campaign
2. **Regimental Association**: Explicit connection to the Leib-Guard Jäger Regiment, one of the most distinguished units of the Russian Imperial Guard
3. **Rarity**: Presentation pieces of this type and quality are exceptionally rare on the market
4. **Completeness**: The object retains its clockwork mechanism and all decorative elements

### 9.1. Attribution

While no maker's mark has been identified in the available photographs, the quality of execution suggests production by a **leading St. Petersburg silversmith** of the period. Possible workshops include:

- The Imperial Cabinet workshops
- Leading independent silversmiths such as the Sazikov or Nichols & Plinke firms

Further examination for hallmarks is recommended.

### 9.2. Valuation Considerations

Objects of this type, when they appear at auction, command significant prices. Comparable regimental presentation silver from the Napoleonic era has achieved:

- £50,000–£150,000 at major auction houses (Christie's, Sotheby's)
- Higher prices for objects with documented Imperial provenance

The combination of clockwork, commemorative inscription, and Imperial Guard association makes this piece particularly desirable to collectors of:
- Russian Imperial decorative arts
- Napoleonic militaria
- Antique horology
- Regimental silver
